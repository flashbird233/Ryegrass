In Australia, ryegrass pollen season spans from September to the end of December each year, with the peak period extending from mid-October to mid-December. Individuals allergic to ryegrass pollen are advised to stay clear of these allergens, especially during peak times, as the best defense is to avoid exposure. After rain, precautions should be taken as water can break down pollen particles, making them smaller and more likely to enter the respiratory tract. Thus, post-rain, those with allergies should ensure they wear masks to minimize inhalation of pollen.
