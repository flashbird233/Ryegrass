Closer Look at Ryegrass

In southeastern Australia, especially areas with temperate grasses, there's a higher risk of thunderstorm asthma, affecting everyday folks. This happens because of things like sensitivity to ryegrass pollen (RGP), seasonal hay fever, and being outside during thunderstorms when pollen is flying. Perennial ryegrass (Lolium perenne), which grows a lot here, plays a big part in this. Even if you've never had asthma before, you could feel your chest tighten during a thunderstorm if you're sensitive to RGP and have hay fever. When thunderstorms hit, RGP pollen grains burst open, releasing tiny allergen-packed bits that are easy to breathe in, making things worse.
